Kuroshio Extension Mooring system

The Kuroshio and Oyashio are the major western boundary currents in the North Pacific Ocean. As they flow eastward after separating from Japan at approximately 35°N and 42°N, the Kuroshio and Oyashio Extension Regions (KOER) have been recognized as “hotspots” for various oceanic processes, including fronts, eddies, and meanders. These processes have significant impacts on ocean dynamics, air-sea interactions, climate system, and marine ecosystems.

The KOE is also one of the regions with the least long-term continuous observations. Since 2015, Ocean University of China (OUC) has started the observation system in the Kuroshio Extension (KE) region and carried out a series of far-reaching scientific-research-oriented in-situ observational experiments. In addition to comprehensive cruises conducted every year, OUC has maintained the Kuroshio Extension Mooring System (KEMS) with 2 surface buoys to the both sides of KE and 5 subsurface moorings across the subtropical water south of KE to the subarctic water in the Oyashio Extension (OE). Meanwhile, OUC along with several cooperative research institutions has developed mobile platforms like Deep Argo floats, a series of drifters and conducted tentative deployments in this region.
